Experience

I’m an interaction designer from Vancouver, Canada. I currently design user experiences and user interfaces at Steamclock Software. I live in the world of product strategy, information architecture, user experience, and graphic design.

At Steamclock Software

At Steamclock, my work involves strategy, information architecture, wire-framing, graphic design, and user interface design. Twice a day, I press fine coffees roasted on the West Coast from Vancouver, Victoria, Portland, and San Francisco. If you’re looking for a crack design and dev team for your next product, or to rev one that already exists, we’re the people to talk to. We are experts in iOS, Android, and the Web.

My Professional Experience

Steamclock Software

UX & UI Designer Dec. 2012 – Present / Vancouver, Canada Area
I design information architecture, wire-frames, interfaces for iOS, Android, and Web apps. I also design digital and analog promo materials; all the things that accompany the launch of an app or website. We’ve shipped multiple projects like Map of the Internet for Peer 1 Hosting to the App Store and Google Play. Checkout the website I designed and developed for Steamclock.

Free Agency Creative

Contract Designer Aug. 2012 – Nov. 2012 / Vancouver, Canada
I handled design and production of point of purchase displays and materials for Nintendo of America, large scale in-mall and direct mail marketing campaigns for Park Royal Shopping Centre, advertising, signage, video and marketing collateral for some of Vancouver’s most successful real estate developers, and print and digital collateral for Marketplace Events – a company that produces some of the largest and longest-running home show events in North America.

Emily Carr University of Art and Design

Interaction Designer Feb. 2012 – Apr. 2012 / Vancouver, Canada
I designed the website for the Emily Carr University Graduation Exhibition Catalogue and directed the deployment of the site to Emily Carr’s servers. Working with a team of students handling the identity of the exhibition, I presented concepts, prototypes, development of HTML, CSS, jQuery, and PHP, throughout the design and production of the catalogue website. The website was designed to make student work easily accessible on any device, on all major browsers. During my research assistantship with ECUAD’s Social + Interactive Media and Stereoscopic 3D Centres, I conducted interviews, composited /images for feature articles, designed motion graphics for promotional and instructional videos, and published content to their respective websites.

My Education
